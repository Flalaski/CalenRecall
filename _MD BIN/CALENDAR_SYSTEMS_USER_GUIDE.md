## Overview

CalenRecall converts all dates to and from a common Julian Day Number (JDN) system, allowing seamless conversion between any calendar systems. When you create an entry, it's stored with a canonical date, but you can view and edit it in any calendar system.

### Calendar Types

Calendars are categorized by their basis:
- **Solar**: Based on the Earth's orbit around the Sun (~365.25 days)
- **Lunar**: Based on the Moon's phases (~29.5 days per month)
- **Lunisolar**: Combines both solar and lunar cycles
- **Other**: Unique systems with different structures

## Solar Calendars

### Gregorian Calendar
- **Type**: Solar
- **Year Length**: 365-366 days
- **Months**: 12 months
- **Era**: CE (Common Era), starts at year 1
- **Leap Years**: Every 4 years, except century years unless divisible by 400
- **Usage**: Most widely used calendar worldwide
- **Best For**: General journaling, modern dates

### Julian Calendar
- **Type**: Solar
- **Year Length**: 365-366 days
- **Months**: 12 months
- **Era**: CE (Common Era), starts at year 1
- **Leap Years**: Every 4 years (simpler than Gregorian)
- **Usage**: Historical dates, Orthodox Christian churches, astronomy
- **Best For**: Historical research, dates before 1582

**Note**: The Julian calendar differs from Gregorian by about 13 days in modern times due to different leap year rules.

### Persian (Jalali/Solar Hijri) Calendar
- **Type**: Solar
- **Year Length**: 365-366 days
- **Months**: 12 months
- **Era**: AH (Anno Hegirae), starts at 622 CE
- **Leap Years**: Complex cycle-based system
- **Usage**: Official calendar of Iran and Afghanistan
- **Best For**: Persian/Iranian cultural dates, modern Middle Eastern dates

### Ethiopian Calendar
- **Type**: Solar
- **Year Length**: 365-366 days
- **Months**: 13 months (12 months of 30 days + 1 month of 5-6 days)
- **Era**: Starts at 8 CE (Gregorian)
- **Leap Years**: Every 4 years
- **Usage**: Official calendar of Ethiopia
- **Best For**: Ethiopian cultural dates

### Coptic Calendar
- **Type**: Solar
- **Year Length**: 365-366 days
- **Months**: 13 months (12 months of 30 days + 1 month of 5-6 days)
- **Era**: AM (Anno Martyrum), starts at 284 CE
- **Leap Years**: Every 4 years
- **Usage**: Coptic Orthodox Church
- **Best For**: Coptic Christian dates, Egyptian cultural dates

### Indian National (Saka) Calendar
- **Type**: Solar
- **Year Length**: 365-366 days
- **Months**: 12 months
- **Era**: Saka Era, starts at 78 CE
- **Leap Years**: Complex cycle-based system
- **Usage**: Official calendar of India (alongside Gregorian)
- **Best For**: Indian cultural dates, official Indian dates

### Thai Buddhist Calendar
- **Type**: Solar
- **Year Length**: 365-366 days
- **Months**: 12 months
- **Era**: BE (Buddhist Era), starts at 543 BCE
- **Leap Years**: Same as Gregorian
- **Usage**: Thailand, some Buddhist communities
- **Best For**: Thai cultural dates, Buddhist religious dates

### Aztec Xiuhpohualli Calendar
- **Type**: Solar
- **Year Length**: 365 days (fixed)
- **Months**: 18 months of 20 days + 5 "nameless" days
- **Era**: Based on cycles, not linear years
- **Usage**: Traditional Aztec calendar system
- **Best For**: Mesoamerican cultural dates, historical Aztec dates

## Lunar Calendars

### Islamic (Hijri) Calendar
- **Type**: Lunar
- **Year Length**: 354-355 days (shorter than solar year)
- **Months**: 12 months
- **Era**: AH (Anno Hegirae), starts at 622 CE
- **Leap Years**: 11 leap years in 30-year cycle
- **Usage**: Islamic religious calendar, some Muslim countries
- **Best For**: Islamic religious dates, Ramadan, Hajj, Islamic holidays

**Note**: The Islamic calendar is about 11 days shorter than the solar year, so Islamic dates shift earlier each solar year.

---

## Lunisolar Calendars

### Hebrew (Jewish) Calendar
- **Type**: Lunisolar
- **Year Length**: 353-355 days (common), 383-385 days (leap)
- **Months**: 12-13 months (leap years have 13 months)
- **Era**: AM (Anno Mundi), starts at 3761 BCE
- **Leap Years**: 7 leap years in 19-year Metonic cycle
- **Usage**: Jewish religious calendar, Israel
- **Best For**: Jewish holidays, Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur, Passover

**Note**: The Hebrew calendar is one of the most complex calendar systems, with intricate rules for leap years and month lengths.

### Chinese Lunisolar Calendar
- **Type**: Lunisolar
- **Year Length**: 353-355 days (common), 383-385 days (leap)
- **Months**: 12-13 months (leap years have 13 months)
- **Era**: Based on cycles, traditionally starts at 2697 BCE
- **Leap Years**: Based on solar terms and lunar cycles
- **Usage**: Traditional Chinese calendar, Chinese New Year, festivals
- **Best For**: Chinese New Year, traditional Chinese dates, Chinese festivals

**Note**: The Chinese calendar is astronomical-based and requires complex calculations. Some approximations may be used for efficiency.

### Baháʼí Calendar
- **Type**: Solar (with named days)
- **Year Length**: 365 days (19 months of 19 days + 4-5 intercalary days)
- **Months**: 19 months
- **Era**: BE (Baháʼí Era), starts at 1844 CE
- **Leap Years**: 4 intercalary days (5 in leap years)
- **Usage**: Baháʼí Faith religious calendar
- **Best For**: Baháʼí religious dates, Baháʼí holidays

## Other Calendar Systems

### Mayan Tzolk'in Calendar
- **Type**: Ritual cycle
- **Length**: 260 days (20 day names × 13 numbers)
- **Structure**: Continuous cycle, not based on years
- **Usage**: Traditional Mayan calendar, divination
- **Best For**: Mayan cultural dates, ritual purposes

### Mayan Haab' Calendar
- **Type**: Solar cycle
- **Length**: 365 days (18 months of 20 days + 5 "nameless" days)
- **Structure**: Continuous cycle
- **Usage**: Traditional Mayan calendar, agricultural purposes
- **Best For**: Mayan agricultural dates, seasonal events

### Mayan Long Count Calendar
- **Type**: Linear count
- **Structure**: Counts days from a fixed epoch (August 11, 3114 BCE)
- **Format**: 5 components (Baktun, Katun, Tun, Uinal, Kin)
- **Usage**: Historical Mayan dates, archaeological dating
- **Best For**: Precise Mayan historical dates, archaeological research

### Cherokee Calendar
- **Type**: Seasonal adaptation
- **Months**: 12 months (adapted to Gregorian structure)
- **Structure**: Traditional Cherokee month names in modern format
- **Usage**: Cherokee cultural calendar
- **Best For**: Cherokee cultural dates, Native American heritage

**Note**: This is a modern adaptation. Consult Cherokee cultural experts for traditional usage.

### Iroquois (Haudenosaunee) Calendar
- **Type**: Lunar-based approximation
- **Months**: 13 moons
- **Structure**: Based on lunar cycles with approximations
- **Usage**: Iroquois cultural calendar
- **Best For**: Iroquois cultural dates, Native American heritage

**Note**: Uses approximations for lunar calculations. Traditional implementation may vary.

## Using Multiple Calendars

### Switching Calendar Systems

1. Open **Preferences** (gear icon in navigation bar)
2. Navigate to **Calendar Settings**
3. Select your preferred **Default Calendar**
4. Optionally enable **Show Multiple Calendars** to see dates in multiple systems simultaneously

### Viewing Entries in Different Calendars

- All entries are stored with canonical dates
- You can view any entry in any calendar system
- The calendar system you choose affects how dates are displayed in:
  - Calendar grids
  - Entry headers
  - Timeline minimap
  - Date navigation

### Creating Entries

- When you create an entry, it uses the currently selected calendar system for display
- The entry is stored with a standard date format internally
- You can later view the same entry in any other calendar system

## Tips and Best Practices

### Choosing a Calendar System

1. **For Modern Dates**: Use Gregorian (default)
2. **For Historical Research**: Use Julian for dates before 1582
3. **For Religious Dates**: Use the appropriate religious calendar (Islamic, Hebrew, etc.)
4. **For Cultural Dates**: Use the calendar system of that culture
5. **For Multiple Contexts**: Enable "Show Multiple Calendars" to see dates in several systems

### Understanding Date Conversions

- Calendar conversions are mathematically precise
- Some calendars have known limitations (see Limitations documentation)
- Negative years are supported for historical dates
- Leap years and intercalary days are handled automatically

### Calendar Accuracy

- Most calendars are fully verified and accurate
- Some calendars use documented approximations for efficiency
- Astronomical calendars (Chinese, Baháʼí) may have minor variations
- See the Limitations documentation for specific details

### Best Practices

1. **Consistency**: Choose one primary calendar for your journal
2. **Documentation**: Note which calendar system you're using for important entries
3. **Conversion**: Use the calendar converter to verify dates when needed
4. **Cultural Sensitivity**: Use appropriate calendars for cultural and religious dates
