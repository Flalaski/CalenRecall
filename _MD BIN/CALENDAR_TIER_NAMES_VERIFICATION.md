## Notes

1. **Calendars without native week concepts**: Mayan Haab', Mayan Long Count, and Aztec calendars use "Period" as a generic term since they don't have a standard subdivision smaller than their 20-day periods.

2. **Mayan Tzolk'in**: Uses "Trecena" for the week tier, which is the correct 13-day period in that calendar system.

3. **Ethiopian and Coptic**: Both use 7-day weeks and now have proper native terms (ሳምንት and ⲥⲁⲃⲃⲁⲧⲟⲛ respectively).

4. **Adapted systems**: Cherokee and Iroquois calendars are modern adaptations, so English terms are used where native terms don't exist or aren't applicable.

5. **Decade equivalents**: Some calendars don't have a native "decade" concept, so approximate terms or closest equivalents are used (e.g., K'atun for Mayan calendars, Xiuhmolpilli for Aztec).
